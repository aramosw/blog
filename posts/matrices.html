Matrix Multiplication Explained

2025-11-04

Given matrices $A \in \mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ and $B \in \mathbb{R}^{n\times p}$, their product $C=AB$ is the $m\times p$ matrix with entries

$$ c_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_{ik} b_{kj}. $$

Example:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 11 & 10 \\ -4 & -5 \end{bmatrix}. $$
